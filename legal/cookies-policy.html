Cookie Policy

We use Cookies to provide you with the best product experience. They not only help us understand how users interact with our products and services, continuously improving the products and services of D/Bond and D/Wallet, but they are also essential for your IT security. This Cookie Policy explains how we use Cookies and why we use them, and it provides information about what cookies you have allowed to use and how to use them, and how to protect your privacy.

• What is a Cookie?

In general, a Cookie is a text file (typically consisting of letters and numbers) that is downloaded to your computer, tablet, smartphone, or other devices (referred to as "device") when you visit our website or use our applications. These files allow us to identify your device and enable us to record certain information as you interact with our website, services, and applications. We use this information to optimize the performance of our website and continuously improve our platform, products, and services. To help you to have a better understanding of the Cookies, their functions, and how you can manage your Cookie settings, we will provide you with a more detailed explanation below.

• Purpose of Using Cookies

We use Cookies to collect and store detailed information about how you use our website and services. This enables us to understand which parts of our website and services received attention and need to further development such parts.

• Types of Cookies We Use 

• Strictly Necessary Cookies

These Cookies are essential for the operation of the website and cannot be disabled in our systems. They are typically set only in response to actions you take that are equivalent to a service request, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling out forms. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these Cookies, but some parts of the website may not run properly if you do so.

• Performance/Analytics Cookies

These Cookies enable us to calculate the number of visits and traffic sources, allowing us to measure and improve the performance of our website. They help us understand the popularity of platform interfaces and how visitors navigate through the platform. All information collected by these Cookies is aggregated and therefore anonymous.

• Security Cookies

Security Cookies and other technologies are used to authenticate your identity, prevent fraud, and protect the security of your data and your property during we providing service to you. Some Cookies also help you to prevent spam, fraud, and abuse.

• Duration of Cookies in Your Browser

 

The length of time that a Cookie stays on your browser device depends on whether it is a “persistent” Cookie or a "session" Cookie. Session Cookies remain on your device until you close your browser. Persistent Cookies, on the other hand, remain on your browsing device until they expire or are deleted.

• Managing Cookies

You can enable or disable Cookie categories by clicking on our Cookie management settings. This includes our own Cookies and Cookies from third-party service providers. You can also enable, disable, or delete Cookies using the browser you use to browse this website. To do so, please follow the instructions provided by your browser (usually found in the “Help,” “Tools,” or “Edit” settings). Please note that if you disable Cookies in your browser settings, you may not be able to access certain services of D/BOND or D/Wallet.

• Cookie Policy Updates

We may update our Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the Cookies we use or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. If we update our Cookie Policy, you will receive a notification when you first visit D/BOND or D/Wallet after the update. If you wish to be informed about the latest changes, you can also revisit this page.

• Contact Us

If you have any questions regarding our Cookie Policy or the processing of your personal data, you could refer to our Privacy Policy or contact us via our email address info@d-labs.org. For certain inquiries, we may require additional verification information from you to ensure that your data is only accessible by yourself.
